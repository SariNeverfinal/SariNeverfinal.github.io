Time Series (Calendar Plot)

We plotted the thefts over time (e.g., monthly and yearly) to identify long-term trends. The results revealed patterns such as increases or decreases over certain periods. In addition, we analyzed seasonal variations in crime rates. We determined if thefts exhibit seasonal trends (e.g., increased theft during the holiday season) and explored potential reasons behind these patterns. For example, spikes in theft during certain months or years could coincide with major events or economic downturns. Lastly, we examined the distribution of crimes across different days of the week. We identified whether thefts are more prevalent on weekends or weekdays and investigated possible explanations.

Map

We mapped the locations of reported crimes to identify spatial patterns and hotspots as well as determine if thefts are concentrated in specific neighborhoods or areas within the city (f.e. rich neighborhoods or touristic areas).
