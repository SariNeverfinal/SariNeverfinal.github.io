Larceny/Theft in San Francisco

Larceny/theft is one of the most common crimes in urban areas, including San Francisco. Between 2003 and 2018, 464.365 counts of larceny/theft were reported in San Francisco.

Insights into Larceny/Theft in San Francisco

Larceny/theft is one of the most common crimes in urban areas, including San Francisco. Utilizing publicly available data from San Francisco's Open Data Portal, specifically the "SF Crime Data" under the public safety category, we analyze trends in larceny/theft between January 1st 2003 to December 31st 2017 to explore potential patterns related to economic conditions, population density, tourist activity, and law enforcement strategies.

We plotted the counts of larceny/theft for every full month between the years 2003 to 2017 to identify long-term trends. The results revealed patterns such as strong increases in larceny/theft over the years. [ADD EXPLANATION]. In addition, we analyzed seasonal variations in crime counts of larceny/theft, but we could not determine strong seasonal trends. Lastly, we examined the distribution of crimes across different days of the week, to identify whether thefts are more prevalent on weekends or weekdays, but the pattern seems even across the week. Most worth noticing is a big spike in larceny/theft on the last weekend of June 2017.

We mapped the locations of reported crimes to identify spatial patterns and hotspots, focusing specifically on theft incidents. Our analysis centered on the average theft count for the year 2017, a period of particular interest due to the highest crime counts in our dataset. This exploration aimed to reveal spatial concentrations of thefts and determine whether they are concentrated in specific neighborhoods or areas within the city, such as affluent neighborhoods or tourist areas.

Our findings indicate that the neighborhoods of Ingleside and Park have the lowest numbers of theft counts, while Southern followed by Northern neighborhoods have the highest numbers of theft counts on average in 2017. This insight provides valuable context for understanding the distribution of theft incidents across different areas of San Francisco. [ADD EXPLANATION]
